Kobe Bryant

Career

  • 1978 - Born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, USA.
  • 1991 - Enrolled at Lower Merion High School and quickly gained national attention as a basketball prodigy.
  • 1996 - Selected 13th overall in the NBA Draft by the Charlotte Hornets, then immediately traded to the Los Angeles Lakers.
  • 1997 - Won the NBA Slam Dunk Contest.
  • 1998 - Became the youngest All-Star Game starter in NBA history at age 19.
  • 2000 ~ 2002 - Won three consecutive NBA Championships with Shaquille O’Neal and the Lakers (three-peat).
  • 2006: Scored 81 points in a single game against the Toronto Raptors.
  • 2008 - Led Team USA to Olympic gold in Beijing
  • 2008 - Named NBA Most Valuable Player
  • 2009, 2010 - Led Lakers to back-to-back championships and won NBA Finals MVP both years.
  • 2012 - Won a second Olympic gold medal at the London Games.
  • 2015 - Announced he would retire at the end of the season.
  • 2016 - Played his final NBA game, scoring 60 points against the Utah Jazz.
  • 2018 - Won an Academy Award (Oscar) for Best Animated Short Film Dear Basketball.
  • 2020 - Kobe, his daughter Gianna, and seven others died in a tragic helicopter crash in Calabasas, California.
  • 2020 - Officially inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame
